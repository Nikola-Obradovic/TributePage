General About the Author

Eiichiro Oda is one of the most famous Japanese Manga Artists in the world, due to his creation of One Piece, the best selling manga in the world, as well as one of the best selling comics in general with over 500 million copies sold. He has inspired many people to make manga themselves and has a huge fanbase for the work that he has put out.

Childhood and Inspiration

Eiichiro Oda was born in Kumamoto, Japan on the 1st of January 1975. He was greatly influenced by anime and manga, especially by another popular anime series called Dragon Ball by Akira Toriyama. Thus Monkey D. Luffy, the main character in One Piece, was inspired by the main character from Dragon Ball, Goku, sharing many similarities in behavior with Goku. Oda also chose to write One Piece to be about pirates because he was inspired by a show called Vicky the Viking.

Romance Dawn

In 1996, Oda made two different versions of a manga called Romance Dawn, which is a story that served as a prototype for One Piece. Afterwards in 1997, Oda started making One Piece and the first chapter as Romance Dawn. The first version of Romance Dawn was made into an adaptation called "One Piece: Romance Dawn Story" and the second version was made into the 907th episode of One Piece, celebrating the 20th anniversary of the series.

Art Style

Oda has a very unique art style with a variaty of characters in One Piece, many of which do not fit into the typical pirate setting. This variaty of characters, as well as the distinctive settings in which the characters of this manga find themselves in, adds a lot of charm to the franchise. Influence of One Piece

One Piece has a huge fanbase, with millions of people waiting each week for a new chapter/episode. It has become so popular that there are 10 statues of the Straw Hat Pirates raised in Japan. One Piece is also featured in many commercials for clothing and other products, as well as in many magazines. There are amusement parks, restaurants and many stores around the world dedicated to One Piece and many people consider it the greatest manga and anime that was ever written.
